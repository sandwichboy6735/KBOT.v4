Strategy Explanations
What each strategy does and when to use it
EMA Crossover
Recommended
The most popular trading strategy in the world. Uses two EMA lines — when the fast line (EMA 9) crosses above the slow line (EMA 21) it signals an uptrend. When it crosses below it signals a downtrend. Works best on trending stocks like NVDA and AAPL.
Best settings: Short EMA 9, Long EMA 21. Works best on 5 min timeframe.
RSI Reversal
Recommended
RSI measures how overbought or oversold a stock is on a scale of 0-100. Below 30 means the stock is oversold and likely to bounce up. Above 70 means it's overbought and likely to fall. Pairs perfectly with EMA for confirmation.
Best settings: Period 14. Keep default RSI levels (30/70).
MACD Signal
Optional
MACD uses the difference between two EMAs to show momentum. When the MACD line crosses the signal line from below it's bullish. From above it's bearish. Great for confirming EMA signals.
Best used alongside EMA Cross as a confirmation filter.
Bollinger Bands
Optional
Three lines: a middle moving average and two outer bands 2 standard deviations away. When price touches the lower band it often bounces up. When it touches the upper band it often falls. Works best in sideways markets.
⚠ BB doesn't work well in strong trending markets — use EMA instead.
VWAP Pullback
Optional
VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the average price weighted by volume. Professional traders use it as a fair value reference. Buying when price dips to VWAP during an uptrend is a high-probability setup.
Best for intraday trading. Resets every day at market open.
Gap & Go
Advanced
Stocks that gap up more than 2% at market open (9:30 AM) often continue moving up. This strategy enters on the first VWAP pullback after the gap. Very high win rate on strong gap days but needs active monitoring.
⚠ Only fires at market open. Need "Pre-market signals" OFF for best results.
Scalp
Advanced
Quick in-and-out trades targeting 1-2% profit. Very short hold times (5-30 minutes). Requires 1 min scan interval and tight stop losses. Not recommended for beginners as it requires fast execution.
⚠ Set scan interval to 1 min for scalping. Needs fast internet connection.
Consensus Setting
Important
This is the minimum number of strategies that must agree before a trade fires. Set to 2 means 2 strategies must both say BUY at the same time. Higher = fewer but higher quality trades. Lower = more trades but more risk.
Recommended: Set consensus equal to half your active strategies. If you have 4 active, set consensus to 2.
⚠ If consensus is HIGHER than your active strategy count, the bot will NEVER trade. This is the #1 reason for no trades!

Risk Management Explained
The most important settings — get these right
Position Size %
8% default
What percentage of your total portfolio to put into each trade. 8% means if you have $1,000 you'll put $80 per trade. Lower = safer but smaller profits. Higher = bigger profits but bigger losses.
$1,000 account at 8% = $80 per trade. $10,000 account at 8% = $800 per trade.
⚠ Never go above 20% per trade — one bad trade could wipe out too much.
Stop Loss %
3% default
If a trade goes AGAINST you by this percentage the bot automatically sells to stop further losses. The most important risk tool. 3% means if you buy at $100, it auto-sells at $97. Without a stop loss one bad trade can wipe out many winning trades.
Buy AAPL at $210. Stop loss at 3% = auto-sell if it drops to $203.70.
⚠ NEVER turn off stop losses when using real money.
Take Profit %
9% default
When a trade goes IN your favor by this percentage the bot automatically sells to lock in the profit. 9% means if you buy at $100 the bot sells at $109. The ratio of take profit to stop loss is crucial — 9% TP / 3% SL = 3:1 ratio.
Buy AAPL at $210. Take profit at 9% = auto-sell at $228.90.
3:1 ratio means you only need to WIN 34% of trades to be profitable!
Max Daily Loss %
5% default
If your account loses this percentage in a single day the bot shuts itself down automatically and stops all trading for the rest of the day. This prevents one bad day from destroying your account. 5% means if you have $1,000 and lose $50 in one day the bot stops.
⚠ This is your safety net. Don't set it above 10%.
Trailing Stop Loss
Optional
A normal stop loss stays fixed. A trailing stop loss moves UP with the price as you profit. So if you buy at $100 with a 3% trailing stop and it goes to $110, your stop loss is now $106.70 — locking in profit. This is one of the most powerful tools for growing accounts.
Buy at $100 → price goes to $120 → trailing stop moves to $116.40 → price drops → sells at $116.40. You keep the profit!
Market Hours Only
Keep ON
When ON the bot only places trades between 9:30 AM and 4:00 PM Eastern Time on weekdays. This is STRONGLY recommended — pre and after-market trading has much lower volume, wider spreads, and more unpredictable price movements.
⚠ If you start the bot before 9:30 AM it will just wait — this is normal! It will kick in automatically at 9:30 AM.

Other Indicators
ATR, ADX, Stochastic, VWAP, Fibonacci — all auto-configured
ATR Period
14 (auto)
Average True Range uses 14 periods — the universal standard. It measures volatility in dollar terms. KBOT uses it to filter out low-volatility periods and confirm breakouts.
ADX Period
14 (auto)
ADX uses 14 periods. Readings above 25 confirm a real trend exists. KBOT automatically gives stronger signals when ADX is above 25 and weaker signals in choppy markets below 25.
Stochastic
14/3 (auto)
Stochastic uses 14 periods for K line and 3 for smoothing. Below 20 = oversold buy zone. Above 80 = overbought sell zone. Works alongside RSI for confirmation.
VWAP
Daily (auto)
VWAP resets every day at market open and recalculates every candle. No settings needed — it automatically weights price by volume. Price above VWAP = bullish. Below = bearish.
Fibonacci
30 bars (auto)
KBOT auto-detects the highest high and lowest low over the last 30 bars and plots the 38.2%, 50% and 61.8% retracement levels. When price is within 1.5% of these levels a signal boost is applied.
Support/Resist
30 bars (auto)
KBOT auto-detects pivot highs and lows over the last 30 bars. When price is within 2% of a support level it adds confidence to buy signals. Near resistance = adds confidence to sell signals.
Z-Score
20 bars (auto)
Measures how many standard deviations the current price is from its 20-bar average. Below -1.5 = statistically cheap, likely to mean-revert up. Above +1.5 = statistically expensive, likely to fall.

Trading Glossary
Plain English explanations of every term in KBOT
EMA
Exponential Moving Average. A line on a chart that shows the average price over a period of time, but gives more weight to recent prices. A 9 EMA reacts faster to price changes than a 21 EMA.
RSI
Relative Strength Index. A number from 0 to 100 showing how overbought (above 70) or oversold (below 30) a stock is. Think of it as a "temperature gauge" for whether a stock has moved too far too fast.
MACD
Moving Average Convergence Divergence. Shows the relationship between two EMAs. When the MACD line crosses above the signal line = bullish. Below = bearish. The histogram shows the strength of the trend.
Candlestick
A way to display price on a chart. Each candle shows 4 prices: Open (where it started), High (highest point), Low (lowest point), Close (where it ended). Green candle = price went up. Red = price went down. The "wick" (thin line) shows the high and low range.
VWAP
Volume Weighted Average Price. The average price of a stock during the day, weighted by how much was traded at each price. Institutional traders (big banks, hedge funds) use VWAP as their benchmark. Price above VWAP = bullish. Below = bearish.
Stop Loss
An automatic sell order that triggers if the price falls below a certain level. It "stops your losses" from getting too big. The most important risk tool in trading. Always use one.
Take Profit
An automatic sell order that triggers when the price reaches your profit target. Locks in gains automatically so you don't have to watch the screen all day.
P&L
Profit and Loss. Your running score for the day. Green = you're up money. Red = you're down money. Day P&L resets every morning at market open.
Win Rate
The percentage of your trades that made money. 60% win rate means 6 out of every 10 trades were profitable. With a 3:1 risk/reward ratio you only need a 34% win rate to be profitable overall.
Sharpe Ratio
Measures how much return you get for the amount of risk you take. Above 1.0 is good. Above 2.0 is excellent. Below 1.0 means your returns don't justify your risk.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough decline in your account value. A 10% max drawdown means at its worst point your account was down 10% from its high. Lower is better.
Bracket Order
A single order that simultaneously places a buy order, a stop loss, AND a take profit. This is what KBOT uses — it places all 3 at once so the exit is automatic no matter which direction the stock goes.
Paper Trading
Trading with fake money that simulates real trading. Alpaca gives you $100,000 of fake money to practice with. Everything behaves exactly like real trading but no real money is at risk. Always paper trade first.
API Keys
A username (API Key ID) and password (Secret Key) that lets KBOT connect to your broker account and place trades automatically. Keep these secret — anyone with your API keys can trade your account.
Consensus
How many of your active strategies must agree before KBOT places a trade. Set to 2 means 2 strategies must both say BUY at the same time. This filters out weak signals and only trades the strongest opportunities.
Backtest
Testing your strategy on historical price data to see how it would have performed in the past. If a strategy had a 65% win rate over the last 90 days in a backtest, it's likely (but not guaranteed) to perform similarly going forward.
